12 Fun and Easy Christmas Crafts for Toddlers

Melbren

Happy holidays guys! Christmas is right around the corner once again, and you're probably about to go through your Christmas shopping list for this year (or maybe already have). But before you make your purchase, why not throw in a couple of fun items to make fun Christmas crafts for toddlers! This is going to be a great bonding activity that your kids will enjoy.

No worries for those who already did their shopping; we'll provide a link to the items so you can just order them online. 

What are some good Christmas crafts for toddlers?

When you search through the web, you can find lots of fabulous Christmas crafts to make. You can find almost anything for all ages. But of course, making Christmas crafts will become more memorable when you make it with your family, especially your kids. They can explore and show their creativity that only kids can do.

So if you're looking for some fun Christmas crafts you can do with our toddler, you've come to the right place. Read on, and we'll show you a bunch of good ones. To get things started, here is our list of compiled Christmas crafts for toddlers ages 2-3.

What crafts can a 2-year-old do?

Most toddlers at the age of 2 now know how to sort by shapes and colors, so making crafts using different shapes and colors will be a good idea. Playing with different textures will be an excellent tactile learning experience too. Here are some easy-to-do crafts you can do with your 2-year-old.

1.  Sticky Wall Christmas Tree Mural

This sticky wall Christmas tree mural by No time for Flashcards will let your toddler decorate a Christmas tree their own way. It's fun and easy to make, and your kids will surely enjoy it. The best part is, you can keep it up for days or even weeks to keep them busy for a few minutes. 

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 10 mins.

What you'll need: 

How to make it?:

Step 1: Cut out a big Christmas tree from your green wrapping paper with your scissors.

Step 2: Grab the pencil, trace the Christmas tree cutout onto your contact paper and cut it.

Step 3: Tape the wrapping paper Christmas tree cutout onto your wall using the painter's tape.

Step 4: Place the contact paper Christmas tree cut out on top of the wrapping paper Christmas tree.

Step 5: Tape the contact paper using the painter's tape. Make sure that you tape the plastic part down and the paper part on the surface.

Step 6: Peel off the paper part of the contact paper to reveal the sticky part underneath. Now you have a sticky surface on your green Christmas tree.

Step 7: You can now let your toddler stick some tissue paper, pompoms, and buttons onto the Christmas tree mural. You can let them crumple the paper to turn them into balls or tear them into smaller pieces.

Step 8: You can help them stick different items onto the tree and teach them about colors and shapes while you're at it.

2. Christmas tree pompom painting

Get your little one's hand busy by letting them paint their own tiny Christmas tree with this Christmas tree pompom painting by Fantastic Fun and Learning. It's a great way to let them explore colors and creativity. Plus, you can hang their artworks as ornaments.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 5-10 mins.

What you'll need: 

How to make it?:

Step 1: Cut Christmas tree shapes on your construction paper.

Step 2: Pour some paint on plastic cups or any shallow containers for easy dipping.

Step 3: Clip-on a pompom ball using a clothespin. The clothespin will act as a handle so your kids can quickly dip the pompom into the paint.

Step 4: Now, let your toddler paint and decorate the Christmas tree cutouts anyway they want!

3. Snowman ball sort

You can't let your toddler play in the snow for too long because of the cold. Luckily, you can still bring home a snowman friend with this Snowman ball sort for toddlers by I Can Teach My Child. No real snow involved, totally safe, and it's great not only for Christmas but all winter long.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Medium

Estimated time: 20 mins.

What you'll need: 

How to make it?:

Step 1: Cut out a huge snowman shape from your cardboard.

Step 2: Trace your shape onto the art paper roll and cut it.

Step 3: Stick the cardboard and the art paper together using the spray adhesive or the glue stick.

Step 4: Find a cup or any circle that will fit the balls and another one slightly bigger. Trace it on your construction paper and cut it, including the middle circle. It's like making a thin donut. It's best if you're construction paper is similarly colored to the balls you'll be using.

Step 5: Use the same cup that fits the ball to trace onto your colored art paper. Trace several circles onto your snowman, depending on how many you want. 

Step 6: Cut the traced circles on the cardboard. You can use an Exacto knife, cutter, or scissors, whatever will be the easiest for you to use.

Step 7: Stick the cut donut papers onto the holes you made on your snowman cardboard.

Step 8: This step is optional. You can draw a scarf outline on your excess art paper or old magazine at home. Cut it and stick it onto your snowman for added color. And to give the snowman friend a scarf.

Step 9: Use the sticks to give your snowman some support and tape them onto the back.

Step 10: Use some zip ties to attach the sticks of the snowman to your basket. 

Step 11: Put the balls in the basket, and you're done!

Step 12: You can now let your toddler play with the new snowman friend by putting the balls through the holes on its body. You can play with them and teach them how to put the balls through the holes' corresponding colors, like the red ball into the red hole.

4. Felt Gingerbread House

Making a real gingerbread house can be really challenging, especially with a toddler (because they might end up eating the house). Good thing you can still make one with this felt gingerbread house by Toddler Approved. It's safe for toddlers, and it's entertaining to make.

Reminder: This may include small decorating pieces, so make sure to supervise them while doing this. If you want, you can replace your embellishments with bigger pieces to avoid choking if they put it in their mouth. Because you know, kids taste everything *sigh*.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Medium

Estimated time: 10-15 mins.

What you'll need: 

How to make it?:

Step 1: Draw and cut out a house shape on your cardboard.

Step 2: Trace the cutout on your felt and cut it too.

Step 3: Repeat steps 1, and if you want more houses to work on.

Step 4: Stick the felt cutout onto the cardboard cut out using a glue stick. Use a different color for the roof.

Step 5: Put a flat, heavy object on top of the cutouts while they're drying. This will prevent them from bending/curling.

Step 6: Once the house cutouts are dry, you can now start decorating it with your kids. Start putting on some tacky glue and stick your embellishments wherever you want them.

Step 7: Wait for it to dry, and you can now display your finished felt gingerbread houses!  

How can I make my toddler fun for Christmas?

You can make Christmas even more fun for your toddlers in many ways. Aside from giving them gifts, you can also make it even more special by doing activities together like playing or making Christmas crafts with them. Not only will it give you a memorable bonding experience for the holidays, but it can also be an excellent learning experience for them.

To spice things up, here are some more crafts you can do with your kids!

5. Mitten Ornament

Decorate your Christmas tree with this fun DIY mitten ornaments by Adventure in a Box you can make with your toddler. It's fun and easy to make, with you and your kid will definitely enjoy.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 10-12 mins

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Draw and cut a mitten shape on your cardstock paper. Don't forget to add a space for your cuff.

Step 2: Cut the inside of the mitten, leaving about 1/4" thick of an outline.

Step 3: Using a clear packaging tape, tape the backside of your mitten, leaving the sticky side facing the front. The inside part of the mitten should have the sticky part.

Step 4: Have your toddler stick sequins and buttons to the sticky surface of your mittens and fill the surface up as much as you can.

Step 5: This step is optional. Fill in any gaps with glitter to achieve a solid color.

Step 6: Attach a cuff on your mitten using a different color cardstock paper. Just cut a rectangle shape and stick it to your mitten.

Step 7: Use a puncher or cut a hole onto your cuff and tie a ribbon through it.

Step 8: You can now hang your mitten ornament on your Christmas tree.

6. Footprint Christmas Tree

Have you ever seen a foot-shaped Christmas tree? If not, why not make one with your little one? This Footprint Christmas Tree by Messy Little Monster is a cute project you can make with your toddler. Just be ready to get a bit messy as they may run off with paint-covered feet.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 5-10 mins

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Pour some paint on a paper plate and have your toddler step on it to cover his/her feet with paint.

Step 2: Have them step on a plain construction paper to get some solid footprints.

Step 3: Repeat steps 1 and 2 until you have as many footprints as you need. Don't forget to wipe or wash off the paint on your toddlers' feet right after if you don't want footprints around your house.

Step 4: Once the footprint is dry, cut them out and decorate them with sequins or paint designs. You can do this with your kid and design as many as you can.

Step 5: Cut out brown rectangles using another construction paper. This will serve as your Christmas tree's trunk.

Step 6: Cut out some stars on yellow construction paper and stick them on top of your footprint Christmas tree. Alternatively, you can just use star-shaped sequins and attach them the same way.

Step 7: Stick your footprint Christmas tree on cardstock paper and use them as decor for your Christmas cards. 

Step 8: You can also stick some strings and hang them as decorations.

7. Cotton Ball Santa

This fun and fluffy cotton ball Santa by Journey to SAHM is an excellent project to work on if your kid loves Santa. It's so easy to make that even our 2-year-old can help out and make them.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: High

Estimated time: 20 mins. + overnight for drying

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Cut out the shapes you'll need to make your Santa using construction paper. A large tan/cream/brown circle for the face, a red hat brim and a small white circle for the pom, and a large half-circle for the body.

Step 2: Assemble the cutout shapes and stick them on a plain construction paper (any color of your choice).

Step 3: Put some glue around the head for Santa's beard and some hair. You can also substitute it with contact paper by sticking it on the surface with the sticky side up.

Step 4: Let your toddler stick the cotton balls onto Santa's head and beard. Allow them to mess up and put the cotton balls anywhere they want to. This will make it more enjoyable for them.

Step 5: Put some googly eyes for Santa's eyes and cut a small red circle, and stick it just below the eyes for Santa's nose.

Step 6: You can now display your fluffy cotton ball Santa! 

8. Handprint Santa Salt Dough Ornament

For your toddlers who are a fan of Santa Claus, here is an excellent project to try! This will mostly need your help in the making, but you can keep the final product as a keepsake of your little one.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: High

Estimated time: 20 mins. + overnight for drying

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Combine your flour, salt, and water into a bowl.

Step 2: Lightly dust a surface with some flour and knead your dough to remove air bubbles. Do this until your dough is smooth. Make sure it's not sticky too.

Step 3: Flatten your dough to about 1/2 inch thick using a rolling pin and cut a piece.

Step 4: Time to call your little one and lightly dust his/her palm with some flour to prevent it from sticking onto the dough.

Step 5: Press their tiny hand lightly onto the dough to make a handprint.

Step 6: Cut the handprint along the edges using a butter knife. You can also poke a hole on one side to attach a string (if you want to use it as an ornament). 

Step 7: Put the handprint dough into the microwave and heat for about 15 seconds.

Step 8: Take it out of the microwave and check if it's still soft to the touch.

Step 9: Put it back into the microwave and heat it for another 20 seconds. You can repeat this step until you feel like the dough is starting to get dry and harden. It would most likely change color too. 

Step 10: Once your dough is dry, you can start painting it. Try painting the entire handprint with white first and let it dry. You can let your toddler paint some pieces too.

Step 11: Leave it overnight to dry and harden further. 

Step 12: Tie a yarn or ribbon onto the tiny hole on the side, and you're done! You can use this as a Christmas tree ornament too.  

How do you make easy Christmas decorations for kids?

Decorating is always a part of Christmas. Besides putting up Christmas trees and other decorations, you can also make your own decorations with your kids. Here are some more crafting ideas for Christmas decorations that are also great for your toddlers.

9. No-mess gingerbread suncatcher ornament

Gingerbread with no crumbs? Yes, we have that. You can also hang these gingerbread ornaments on your window or your Christmas tree! This no-mess gingerbread suncatcher ornament by Teaching 2 and 3-year-olds is an excellent addition to your holiday decorations.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 10-15 mins

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Draw a gingerbread pattern on your paper plate or cardstock paper. Cut it out so you'll have a gingerbread-shaped hole in the middle.

Step 2: Cut your contact paper. The piece should be big enough to cover the gingerbread-shaped hole. The sticky side should be on one side of the paper.

Step 3: Have your toddler stick on the small tissue paper squares onto the sticky side of the contact paper. Make sure to cover the whole gingerbread hole.

Step 4: When the gingerbread hole is covered with tissue paper, get another piece of contact paper, and cover it. The two sticky parts should be facing each other.

Step 5: Once you got your paper covered, cut around the edges of the gingerbread hole. Leave about 1/2 centimeter thick to serve as an outline for your suncatcher.

Step 6: Punch a hole on top of the gingerbread and thread yarn through it. Tie the yarn to create a loop. You can use this to hang your suncatcher.

Step 7: Put a double-sided tape on the gingerbread parts where you'll put the eyes and other details.

Step 8: Have your kid stick on the eyes and other details.

Step 9: You can now hang your finished gingerbread suncatcher!

10. Paper Ornament

Have your toddlers create and decorate their own ornaments with this paper ornament craft by Glitter on a Dime.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 5-10 mins.

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Cut a big circle from your paper plate.

Step 2: Have the kids decorate each circle. They can use different objects like markers, paper cut-outs, foam stickers, beads, etc. Let them be creative and design it however they want to.

Step 3: Punch a hole onto the circles, thread a ribbon through it and tie it up. This will serve as a loop for hanging.

11. Christmas wreath

Another easy-to-do decor you can make with your little one is this Christmas wreath by Crafts on Sea. If you did craft number 10 or the paper ornaments, you could use the other part of the paper plate cut out for this project.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 5-10 mins.

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Grab a paper plate and cut out a circle in the middle part. You should have a paper plate ring.

Step 2: Spread some glue all over the paper plate ring.

Step 3: Have your toddler stick on some torn and crumpled green tissue paper pieces and stick them onto the paper plate rings. 

Step 4: Add some different colored tissue paper as well to create some design.

Step 5: Puch a hole on one side. Thread and tie some yarn to serve as a loop for hanging.

Step 6: Once it's done, you can now display your artwork!

12. Popsicle Reindeers

Christmas wouldn't be complete without Santa's reindeers. Make a bunch of them with your toddler using popsicle sticks! This popsicle reindeer by Happy Hooligans will surely add a unique ornament to your Christmas decorations.

Best for ages: 2 and up

Difficulty: Low

Estimated time: 5-10 mins.

What you'll need:

How to make it?:

Step 1: Grab a popsicle and paint it brown.

Step 2: Get some brown pipe cleaners and cut them in half.

Step 3: Get half of the pipe cleaner and fold it in half. Make sure to make some wavy ends for the antlers.

Step 4: Stick the pipe cleaner onto the painted popsicle stick using a hot glue gun. An adult should do this step as a hot glue gun can be dangerous to your kid. 

Step 5: Stick on the googly eyes and the pompom nose onto your reindeer. 

Step 6: You can also stick a piece of string onto the back for hanging. And you're reindeer is done!
